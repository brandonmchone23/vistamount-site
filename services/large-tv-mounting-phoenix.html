The Problem

A 100-lb TV needs more than two drywall anchors.

Standard TV mounts come with bolts that attach to two studs. That's fine for a 50″ TV. For an 85-inch, you're hanging 130+ lbs from two screws — well within safety margins on paper, but a long way from how you actually want a TV that costs $3000-$8000 supported. The right approach for large TVs is a commercial-grade mount rated well above the TV's weight, lagged directly into the wall studs — every bolt sunk into the center of framing, verified with a wall scanner before a single hole is drilled. The load lives in the studs, where it belongs, and the mount doesn't move.

Common Questions

FAQs

Is my drywall strong enough for an 85-inch TV?
Drywall is never strong enough on its own — the load goes into studs, not drywall. As long as your studs are standard 16-inch or 24-inch on center wood framing, we can mount any TV up to 100"+ safely. For the largest panels we use mounts that span multiple studs, with every lag sunk directly into framing.
Do you supply the mount or do I?
Either. We recommend mounts that are over-rated for your TV's weight (e.g., a 150-lb mount for a 100-lb TV). If you'd rather we source it, just tell us your TV model when you book.
Can you mount an 85-inch on a fireplace?
Yes, but with caveats. The fireplace wall has to actually have studs we can anchor into (some have a metal studded wall over a brick chimney that limits options). Heat clearance is critical — if your fireplace runs hot, mounting any TV that close to the heat source will cook the panel. Send us a photo and we'll tell you if your setup will work.
How long does an 85-inch install take?
2-3 hours for a standard wall, longer on unusual walls or when adding in-wall concealment at the same time.
Can you install 90-inch and 98-inch TVs too?
Yes — same approach scales up. The largest panels (98″ and up) are usually delivered to a custom space (theater room, garage, patio bar) and we'll often coordinate with whoever's framing the space if it's new construction.
