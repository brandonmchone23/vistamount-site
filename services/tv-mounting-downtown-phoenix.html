The Problem

High-rise walls eat regular installers alive.

Mounting a TV in a downtown high-rise isn't the same job as a single-family home in Chandler. Behind the paint you'll find poured concrete, CMU block, or steel studs — and a standard wood-stud install kit is useless on all three. Drywall anchors pull straight out of metal framing. Wood lags won't bite concrete. Add an HOA that says the walls stay intact, a freight elevator that needs booking, and a lease you'd like your deposit back on, and most handymen are out of their depth before they've opened the toolbox. We bring the right anchors for every downtown wall type, hardware rated well beyond your TV's weight, and concealment options that don't require opening walls. We've done this enough times to know which buildings have which walls before we arrive.

Common Questions

FAQs

Can you mount a TV on concrete or block walls?
Yes. Poured concrete and CMU block are common in downtown buildings, and we carry hammer drills, Tapcons, sleeve anchors, and wedge anchors rated well beyond any TV's weight. Concrete is actually one of the strongest substrates you can mount to — it just takes the right hardware and tooling, which is exactly what most handymen don't carry.
Do you mount TVs in downtown apartments and condos?
Yes — high-rise and condo installs are one of our most common jobs. Most installs are fully reversible. If your lease doesn't allow drilling, we offer freestanding TV setups and on-wall raceways for cord concealment that come off cleanly when you move out, plus move-in / move-out dismount and reinstall service.
How fast can you get to downtown Phoenix?
Same-day slots are available most days in 85004 and 85003 — we're based downtown, so you're not waiting on someone driving in from the East Valley. Book online and you'll see real available openings, including same-day when the schedule allows.
Do I need HOA approval to mount a TV?
Depends on your building. Most downtown HOAs allow TV mounting on interior walls but restrict in-wall modifications. We work within your building's rules — surface concealment where in-wall isn't permitted — and we document wall condition before and after so there's never a question at move-out.
How do you find studs in a high-rise wall?
We use a Walabot in-wall scanner and thermal imaging to map what's actually behind the drywall — steel studs, conduit, plumbing — before drilling a single hole. In buildings we've worked before, we usually know the wall construction before we even arrive.
