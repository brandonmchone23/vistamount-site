The Problem

Outdoor isn't just “indoor, but outside.”

A regular indoor TV outside fails fast: heat damage, sun bleach, monsoon dust through the vents. Even outdoor-rated TVs need the right mount (rust-proof, sun-rated), the right placement (no direct afternoon sun, no west-facing screens in summer), and weather-sealed cable runs. Most handymen will mount whatever you bought and walk away — we'll tell you if your TV or your spot is going to fail before we put a hole in your stucco.

Mount: Yours or Ours

If you bought a weather-rated mount (we recommend SunBriteTV, Peerless, or Strong outdoor lines), we'll install it. If you'd rather we source one, we'll pick a mount rated for your TV's size and your wall type.

02

Sun & Glare Check

Before we drill, we look at where the sun hits your patio in summer afternoons. If your screen will be unwatchable from 2-6 PM, we'll tell you — better to know before than after.

03

Stucco & Block Wall Anchors

Stucco-over-frame uses studs behind. Solid CMU block walls take Tapcons or sleeve anchors. Either way, we seal every penetration so monsoon rain doesn't find its way behind the mount.

04

Weather-Sealed Cable Runs

Power and signal are routed through outdoor-rated conduit or sealed wall penetrations. Drip loops on every connection. No cable ever sits in a puddle after a rainstorm.

05

Soundbar & Streaming

We can pair your outdoor TV with a weather-rated soundbar, run audio cable in the same conduit, and set up a streaming device that survives the heat (Apple TV runs cooler than most Roku/Fire sticks).

06

Cover & Power-Down Habits

If you have a TV cover, we'll show you how to use it right. If you don't, we'll tell you when to consider one. Same with power: leaving an outdoor TV on standby in 110° heat shortens its life.

Common Questions

FAQs

Do you supply the outdoor mount or do I buy it?
Both options. Most customers prefer to buy their own (Amazon has good options for $80–$300 depending on size). If you'd rather we source it, we'll pick a weather-rated mount that matches your TV and add it to the invoice. Just text us your TV model and we'll recommend something.
Will my regular indoor TV survive on a covered patio?
Maybe, but probably not for long. Indoor TVs aren't sealed against dust or sized for high heat. If your patio is fully covered, shaded, and you bring the TV in during summer monsoons, an indoor model can last a couple of years. If it's exposed at all, get an outdoor TV (SunBriteTV, Furrion, or similar) — they're built for it.
Where in Phoenix do you install outdoor TVs?
Anywhere in the metro — Phoenix proper, Scottsdale, Chandler, Tempe, Mesa, Gilbert, Glendale, Peoria, Surprise. We've done patio TVs poolside, on covered ramadas, and on stucco walls.
My TV will face west. Is that a problem?
In the summer, yes. Direct afternoon sun on a west-facing screen will wash it out from about 2 PM until sunset. Some outdoor TVs have high-brightness panels that handle this (3000+ nits) — standard TVs don't. If your spot is west-facing, we'll tell you what to expect before we install.
Can you run power and HDMI to a patio that doesn't have outlets?
Yes. We can either fish through the wall to an indoor outlet on the other side (cleanest method), or run a sealed conduit along the wall to the nearest outdoor receptacle. If you have no outdoor outlets at all, we'd recommend having an electrician add one before we install.
How long does an outdoor install take?
Standard outdoor TV with simple cable run: 1.5–2.5 hours. Add 30–60 minutes if we're routing through stucco, sealing block walls, or hiding cable in conduit. We'll give you a time estimate when you book.
