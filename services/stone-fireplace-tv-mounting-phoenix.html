The Problem

One wrong drill bit and you're paying a mason.

Natural stone, faux stacked stone, and brick all behave differently under a drill. Solid masonry needs hammer drills with carbide bits and toggle bolts or sleeve anchors rated for the wall. Faux veneer over framing needs you to find the studs *behind* the stone first. Tile is the trickiest of all — you have to drill the grout line, not the tile, and use the right anchor for the substrate underneath. Most handymen and big-box install services either won't try, or try and crack something.

01

Wall Type Diagnosis

We start by determining what you actually have: solid masonry, manufactured stone over framing, brick veneer, or tile over backer board. Each one gets a different anchor strategy.

02

Right Anchor for the Substrate

Tapcon screws for solid masonry. Sleeve anchors or wedge anchors for heavy loads. Toggle bolts when there's a hollow cavity. Epoxy anchors for the trickiest stone.

03

Slow, Clean Holes

We drill on low speed with steady pressure, never high-speed hammering on visible stone. The hole is exactly the anchor diameter — no oversized, no chipped edges.

04

On-Wall or In-Wall Cord Hiding

In-wall concealment isn't safe for solid masonry, so we use a paint-matched raceway that disappears against the stone, or fish power down to the firebox if there's a chase.

05

Heat Considerations

If your fireplace runs hot, we check whether the TV's mounting position will put it in the heat plume. If it will, we'll recommend a different position or a heat shield before we drill anything.

Common Questions

FAQs

Will you crack my stone?
Not if we do it right. We use the correct hammer drill, the correct bit, and we take it slow on visible faces. If we ever did damage stone, our $1M general liability insurance covers the repair — but it hasn't happened on a Phoenix install.
Can you hide the cords behind the stone?
Usually not behind solid stone — cutting into masonry is a separate trade. But we have two clean options: route a paint-matched raceway down the surround that's barely visible from a few feet away, or fish power down to a hidden outlet near the firebox if there's a cavity to fish through.
My fireplace is faux stacked stone over drywall. Different process?
Yes — faux stone is usually adhered to a substrate over standard framing. We find the studs behind the stone with a deep-scan stud finder, then drill through the stone into the studs. No need for masonry anchors in this case.
Brick fireplace vs. stone — any difference?
Brick is generally easier than natural stone. Standard Tapcons or sleeve anchors work great in solid brick. We drill through the mortar joint when possible (easier to patch than brick face) and use the brick face only when the load requires it.
Can you mount on slate, marble, or polished tile?
Yes, but we drill the grout line whenever possible. Drilling polished stone or tile face requires a diamond bit and a steady hand — doable, but adds time. We'll discuss the approach before we start.
